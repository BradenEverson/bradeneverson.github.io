Physics Informed IoT Simulations in Rust

Braden Everson

Published on September 5, 2024

One of the things I have always been super interested in is the world of physical simulations. On top of this, I've loved IoT devices ever since I first got into programming. One of the first projects I ever made was some very hacky Jack-O-Lantern that used the Google Cloud Voice Recognition service to dispense candy when people said "trick or treat" into it. As my skills in Rust developed, I found myself very interested in seeing if I could create something I've always wanted to: a system of modular peripherals that could be visualized through a 3D render dependent on the peripheral readings.


For example, this could take the form of a physical plane armed with a gyroscope and accelerometer. As pitch and poll vary, a 3D render of the plane would respond accordingly. This has many real-world applications, but truthfully I enjoy the silly ones.
